Section 01

Why conduct structured supplier meetings?

Rather than issuing a generic RFQ to unknown vendors, WEG adopted a structured engagement model — holding individual technical meetings with each supplier before pricing. This approach generates concrete, measurable value.

🎯
Technical fit verified before money is spent
Each supplier had to demonstrate real capability for the specific requirements: hipot (up to 4 kV), functional test via RS-485/Modbus, load test in a fixed 50 °C cabin, and automated electrical contacting. Gaps — like active load capability or LabVIEW-only software stacks — were surfaced in the meeting, not after contract signature.
✓ Risk reduction
🌎
Brazil commissioning requirement enforced early — saving months
Brazil on-site commissioning and post-sale support is a hard requirement. Two suppliers (JR Automation — Hitachi, and Develop LLC — USA) were disqualified before any commercial effort was invested. This saved both parties significant time and preserved the shortlist integrity. Finding this constraint after full quotations would have cost 3–4 months.
✓ Process efficiency
📐
Scope clarifications that prevent costly rework
Key technical decisions were locked in through supplier dialogue: HMI test is not required (PLC communicates via RS-485/Modbus directly); load cabin is temperature-only at fixed 50 °C with no humidity; rheostatic load (no regen to grid) is sufficient; hipot contacts only power terminals, not control terminals. These four clarifications alone prevent scope creep that would add weeks of revision to any received quotation.
✓ Scope control
🔬
Market intelligence unavailable from a datasheet
Meetings revealed that BBS Automation (Germany/China) has 18-station inverter EOL test + 10-station assembly lines in production; MASMEC (Italy) has delivered direct automotive inverter hipot + functional + load test benches; Bosch Manufacturing Innovation already built 5 projects for WEG teams and their first external WEG line dates to 2014. This intelligence is unavailable from websites or brochures.
✓ Market intelligence
💰
Competitive pressure and a meaningful price spread already visible
With only the first two quotations received, there is already a significant price differential between suppliers (detailed in Section 04). Having multiple qualified suppliers in parallel creates negotiating leverage and allows WEG to benchmark pricing against the European, Brazilian, and Chinese markets simultaneously.
✓ Commercial leverage
🤝
WEG component integration confirmed supplier by supplier
Every supplier was explicitly asked about WEG PLCs, drives, contactors, and relays — a strategic requirement. Results ranged from confirmed compatibility (Bosch MFI, who buys more than 1 million WEG products per year) to open items under investigation (BBS: CODESYS-based WEG PLC; MASMEC: Siemens default being evaluated). This cannot be assessed without direct technical dialogue.
✓ Strategic alignment
📅
Timeline realism confirmed from multiple independent sources
The target of supplier selection in July–August 2026, PO by end-2026, and commissioning in Brazil by end-2027 was shared with every supplier. Capacity constraints (BBS flagged this as their primary concern), local visa and travel logistics (BBS needs WEG invitation letters), and FAT/SAT timelines were all scoped in real conversations.
✓ Schedule confidence
📊
Inbound interest — FFT Produktionssysteme proactively reached out
FFT (Fulda, Germany) reviewed WEG’s published specifications, prepared a formal Q&A Excel document, attached an NDA, and requested a meeting — all without being contacted first. This demonstrates the quality of WEG’s technical package and adds a strong European candidate to the shortlist at no outreach cost.
✓ Brand & technical credibility

Section 05

Gains & potential gains from this process

A summary of value already generated and value expected from the structured supplier engagement model.

💡

Early disqualifications saved 3–4 months

Develop LLC (USA) and JR Automation were removed from the process before any commercial effort was invested. Finding this constraint post-quotation would have added months of wasted cycle time for both parties.

📐

Scope locked — zero design rework risk on 4 key points

HMI test not required. Hipot contacts power terminals only. Load cabin is 50 °C fixed, no humidity. No regen to grid. These four clarifications alone prevent scope creep that would add weeks of revision to any quote.

📊

~25% price spread already visible with just 2 quotes

BBS € 728K vs Bosch ≈ € 973K equivalent. With 6–7 more quotes incoming, competitive benchmarking will enable well-founded budget definition and a stronger negotiating position.

🌎

Global market coverage — 4 geographies simultaneously

Parallel evaluation of Europe (3 suppliers), Brazil (2), China (3), and USA (2) ensures WEG is not limited to regional pricing or capabilities. A China-origin supplier may deliver the best technical reference at the lowest cost.

🛡️

Risk catalogue built for the contracting phase

Known risks documented: WEG PLC compatibility (BBS, MASMEC), software language (LabVIEW default at 2 suppliers), Brazil visa logistics (BBS), capacity risk (BBS). These become explicit contract clauses rather than surprises during execution.

🤝

WEG reciprocity advantage identified with Bosch

Bosch MFI holds an exclusive supply contract with WEG Autação, purchasing >1 million WEG products per year. Choosing Bosch reinforces this strategic commercial relationship beyond the scope of this project.
