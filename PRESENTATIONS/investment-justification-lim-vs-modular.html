The Disqualifier

Why LIM is not a real option

Three of LIM's limits are not about throughput or price — they are hard technical ceilings that rule it out for the ORION program entirely.

🚫

No portfolio coverage

LIM cannot test CFW900, CFW11 or CFW500 — the core drives of the ORION program. No quantity of LIM units changes this.

⚡

Under-rated current

LIM tops out at ~39 A. The ORION-class drives require up to 60 A under load — beyond LIM's electrical capability.

🧱

Fixed, non-modular

LIM is locked at 288/day with all three tests in one combined module. It cannot grow with demand and the combined module is a single point of failure.

The Economics

Two financial advantages of modularity

The headline price is higher, but the cost structure is materially better.

📉

The marginal cost collapses

Base system: R$6.0M ÷ 274 = R$21,898 per product/day. An added load module: R$1.65M ÷ 137 = R$12,044 per product/day — roughly half. The more you scale, the cheaper each unit of capacity becomes. At full 959/day the blended rate is ~R$14,859 per product/day.

🗓️

Capex follows demand

Spend ~R$6M at launch, then add R$1.65M modules only as ORION volume ramps Y1→Y5. Capital stays aligned with revenue instead of committing R$14.25M up front for capacity that sits idle for years.

Beyond the Numbers

Operational & strategic gains

🤖
Automated electrical connection
Replaces LIM's manual fixture connection — improving cycle-time repeatability, eliminating operator wiring errors, reducing labor cost, and removing manual handling of energized fixtures (safety + ergonomics).
🧩
Resilience through separate modules
If one load module is down for maintenance, the line keeps producing on the others. LIM's combined hipot+functional+load module is a single point of failure — a fault stops all testing.
🔮
Future-proof for the portfolio
60 A capacity and CFW900/CFW11/CFW500 coverage mean the line supports the current and next generation of drives — protecting the investment well beyond ORION.
📊
No stranded capital
Capacity is purchased in R$1.65M increments matched to demand, avoiding both over-investment (idle LIM units) and the under-capacity risk of a fixed system.

Approve Modular All Drives
